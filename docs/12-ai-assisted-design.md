# AI-Assisted Design

## Overview

AI tools were used as a support tool during the design process of NoteFlow.

The goal was not to let AI replace the designer, but to use AI as a design assistant for ideation, UX thinking, writing, documentation, and review.

The final design decisions, Figma screens, visual style, components, and design system were reviewed and adjusted manually.

---

## Role of AI in This Project

AI was used to support the following parts of the project:

* Ideation
* UX research planning
* Proto-persona structure
* User flow review
* UX writing
* Accessibility review
* Design system organization
* Developer handoff planning
* GitHub documentation

---

## AI-Assisted Design Principle

The main principle was:

**AI helps generate and organize ideas, but the designer makes the final decisions.**

For NoteFlow, AI helped me think faster and organize the project better, but I still had to decide what was useful, what matched the user needs, and what should be designed in Figma.

---

## Research-Based Use of AI

The use of AI was connected to the UX process.

The project started with a beginner user problem:

Beginner music students may struggle with note reading and may need short, simple, and motivating practice.

AI was used to explore this problem from different UX angles, but the final structure was based on the project’s UX foundations, affinity diagram, persona, user flow, and IA.

---

## 1. AI for Ideation

AI was used to brainstorm possible features for a beginner note-reading practice app.

Possible ideas included:

* Short daily practice
* Multiple-choice note recognition
* Instant feedback
* Weak note review
* Progress tracking
* Practice streak
* Weekly accuracy chart
* Recommended practice

### Design Decision

Not every AI suggestion was used.

The selected features were the ones that matched the main UX findings:

* Users need short practice.
* Users need clear feedback.
* Users need to know what to review.
* Users may feel motivated by progress.

Because of this, NoteFlow focuses on short practice, feedback, weak notes, and progress tracking.

---

## 2. AI for UX Research Planning

AI was used to help create possible research questions for future user interviews.

Example research questions:

* Which part of note reading is most difficult for you?
* Do you prefer short or long practice sessions?
* What kind of feedback helps you after a wrong answer?
* Does seeing progress make you more motivated?
* Which notes do you confuse most often?
* What would make a note-reading app feel less stressful?

### Design Decision

The project currently uses proto-research and assumption-based UX analysis.

AI helped organize possible research directions, but real user interviews would still be needed in a future version to validate the assumptions.

---

## 3. AI for Persona and Empathy

AI was used to help structure the proto-persona for the target user.

The persona represents a beginner music student who:

* Wants to recognize notes faster
* Feels confused by some notes
* Needs short practice
* Wants clear feedback
* Feels motivated by visible progress

### Design Decision

The final persona was used to guide design decisions such as:

* Keeping the Practice Screen focused
* Making feedback supportive
* Using a calm visual style
* Showing progress clearly

---

## 4. AI for User Flow Review

AI was used to review whether the main app flow was simple and logical for a beginner user.

The final flow became:

**Welcome → Home → Practice → Result → Progress**

### Design Decision

This flow was selected because it follows the user’s learning journey:

1. Understand the app
2. Start practice
3. Answer questions
4. Receive feedback
5. Check progress

AI helped review the flow, but the final order was chosen based on the UX goal of keeping the experience simple and beginner-friendly.

---

## 5. AI for UX Writing

AI was used to explore short and clear microcopy for the app.

Examples:

* Start Practice
* View Progress
* Great job!
* Practice Again
* Review Weak Notes
* Track your improvement

### Design Decision

The final UX writing style is simple, supportive, and beginner-friendly.

This supports the persona because beginner users should not feel stressed or judged while practicing.

---

## 6. AI for Accessibility Review

AI was used to review basic accessibility considerations.

The review focused on:

* Readable text
* Clear hierarchy
* Large tappable elements
* Feedback that does not rely only on color
* Progress shown with both visuals and numbers
* Simple navigation

### Design Decision

Based on this review, the design includes:

* Clear button hierarchy
* Large answer options
* Visual and numeric progress
* Correct and wrong answer states
* Calm and readable layouts

In a future version, accessibility should be improved with real contrast checks and user testing.

## AI Tools Used

AI tools were used for:

---

## What AI Did Not Do

AI did not replace the design process.

AI did not:

* Make all final design decisions
* Replace user research
* Replace usability testing
* Automatically create the final Figma design
* Decide the final visual system alone
* Validate the product with real users

These parts still require human design judgment and future user testing.

---

## Reflection

Using AI helped me work faster and think more clearly about the product design process.

It was especially helpful for organizing ideas, improving UX writing, preparing documentation, and reviewing design decisions.

However, I learned that AI is most useful when the designer already has a clear problem, target user, and design goal.

In this project, AI supported the process, but the final design decisions were based on the UX needs of beginner music students and were manually applied in Figma.

---

## Interview Summary

In an interview, I would explain my use of AI like this:

I used AI as a design assistant, not as a replacement for my own design decisions. For NoteFlow, AI helped me organize ideas, create research questions, improve UX writing, review accessibility, and structure the documentation. But the final user flow, Figma screens, visual style, components, and design system were designed and adjusted manually by me.
