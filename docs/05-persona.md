# Proto-Persona

## Overview

A persona is a fictional but realistic representation of the target user.

For NoteFlow, this persona is a proto-persona. It is based on assumptions about beginner music students and should be validated later with real user research.

---

## Persona

### Name

Sara

### Age

17

### Role

Beginner piano student

### Experience Level

Beginner

---

## Background

Sara has recently started learning piano and basic music theory. She understands that note reading is important, but she still needs time to recognize notes on the music staff.

She practices with her teacher and sometimes uses paper exercises, but she wants a simpler and more motivating way to practice on her phone.

---

## Goals

Sara wants to:

* Recognize notes faster
* Feel more confident during piano lessons
* Practice note reading regularly
* Understand which notes she needs to review
* See her progress over time

---

## Pain Points

Sara struggles with:

* Recognizing notes quickly
* Confusing some notes on the staff
* Feeling bored by long exercises
* Feeling stressed when she makes mistakes
* Not knowing exactly which notes she should practice more

---

## Needs

Sara needs:

* Short and simple practice sessions
* Clear multiple-choice answers
* Instant feedback
* Supportive messages after mistakes
* Weak note review
* Progress tracking
* A calm and beginner-friendly interface

---

## Motivation

Sara feels more motivated when practice feels achievable. She likes seeing small progress, such as accuracy, streaks, and weekly improvement.

She does not want the app to feel like a difficult exam. She wants it to feel like a helpful practice tool.

---

## User Scenario

Sara opens NoteFlow after her piano lesson. She wants to practice note reading for a few minutes.

She starts a short practice session, answers multiple-choice note questions, receives feedback, and then checks her result.

After practice, she sees her weak notes and uses the Progress Screen to understand how she is improving.

---

## Design Implications

Based on Sara’s needs, NoteFlow should:

* Make the Start Practice button easy to find
* Keep each practice session short
* Show one question at a time
* Give clear correct/wrong feedback
* Show weak notes after practice
* Display progress in a simple and motivating way
* Use a calm visual style with clear hierarchy

---

## Summary

Sara represents a beginner music student who wants to improve note reading without feeling overwhelmed.

The design of NoteFlow focuses on helping users like Sara practice in a simple, short, supportive, and motivating way.
